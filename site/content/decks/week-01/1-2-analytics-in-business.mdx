<Slide title="Business Analytics">

- “The road to analytics success is a slippery one” ~ Forbes
- Companies are increasingly adopting data analytics as a core priority
  - Data driven decision-making is the gold standard
- Implementation lags intent – value realization gap
- Generative AI adoption has taken off
  - 75% of surveyed firms are using AI in some capacity1

</Slide>

<Slide title="Uses of Data Analytics in Business">

- Data-driven decision-making
- Improved operations
  - Improving efficiency of existing business lines
  - Reducing product and administrative costs
  - Supply chain optimization
- Better customer understanding
  - Demand estimation
  - Customized product offerings and support provision
- Risk assessment/management
- Regulatory compliance

</Slide>

<Slide title="In-house vs Third-party">

- In-house solutions are more flexibility for more cost
  - Requires a team or the mythical “full-stack” analyst
- Many companies offering analytics services
  - BI SaaS solutions such as Tableau, PowerBI
  - Turnkey analytics solutions
- Trade-off often made based on size and workforce
- You should be prepared to either produce or consume analytic outputs

</Slide>

<Slide title="value realization gap">

- Desire to implement data analytics is ubiquitous
- Ability to do so is not, creating the value gap
- Common frictions to adoption:
  - Technology learning curve
  - Employee adoption
  - C-level acceptance
  - Implementation difficulty
  - Data availability

</Slide>

<Slide title="Data Analytics Limitations">

- Technical limitations
  - Modeling assumptions
  - Data availability
  - Modeling error
- Human limitations
  - Understanding / trust
  - Adoption costs
  - Job security

</Slide>
