<Slide title="Accounting has always been analytics">

- Every branch of accounting has been doing data analysis since the beginning
  - Double entry bookkeeping is an error-correcting code
- Analytical methods have advanced, increasing the potential of Accounting knowledge and data

</Slide>

<Slide title="Gamut of analytics in accounting">

- Acquisition: collecting and measuring data
- Cleaning: ensuring the quality of the data, audits
- Storage: could just be excel, but hopefully not!
- Combination: combining multiple data sources
- Analysis: modeling the economics
- Data driven decisions: using analytical conclusions to inform decision-makers
- Communication: conveying accounting insights

</Slide>

<Slide title="Accounting Data">

- Accounting is the language of business, accounting data is the data of business
- Companies have myriad systems and datasets, Accountants often are the ones that understand the relations between them all
- Accountants ensure data quality, a “ground truth”

</Slide>

<Slide title="Accounting Analytical Models">

- Simple: financial statement analysis is simple models (often just ratios) that capture fundamentals
- Moderate: aggregating transactions in the general ledger to produce financials and provide drill-downs
- Advanced: default prediction for doubtful accounts

</Slide>

<Slide title="Aiding Decision-making">

- Accountants measure almost all aspects of a firm
- These data are necessary for business decisions
- These data are also often the “target”
  - E.g., managers may try for EPS or EBITDA targets
- Accounting insight is necessary to understand the data and inform/suggest decisions

</Slide>

<Slide title="Where you fit in">

- Regardless of your analytics chops, your domain expertise will always be valuable
  - Don’t underestimate how impactful knowing accounting can be in DA conversations
  - Don’t overestimate how much others know about accounting, you’re the expert!
- Play to your strengths

</Slide>
