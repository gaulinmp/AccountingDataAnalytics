<Slide title="Analytics in Tax">

- What-if analyses for tax planning (e.g. tax credits, bonus depreciation, etc.)
- Separate the sources of tax burden (segment, geographic, category, etc.)
- Industry comparison of effective tax rates
- Analyze and explain GAAP vs tax income differences
- Estimate risk of tax positions
- Transfer pricing schedule optimization

</Slide>

<Slide title="Data for Tax Analytics">

- Financial reporting accounting data
  - Often does not have sufficient information for tax use
  - Tax departments often have customized “pull” of data
- Tax schedules & regulations
- Personal tax information (W-2, 1099, K-1)

</Slide>

<Slide title="Tax Compliance">

- Risk inherent in operating in multiple jurisdictions
- Analysis of all transactions instead of sampling approach reduces risk
- Tax authorities employ models to select audit targets
  - Companies are mimicking these models to stay ahead

</Slide>

<Slide title="Analyzing Tax Liability">

- Valuation allowance assessment requires estimating the realizability of deferred tax assets
- Uncertain tax positions require estimating probability for “more likely than not” recognition threshold
- Comparing expected and actual sales tax paid
- Tracking customer location for online sales

</Slide>

<Slide title="Tax planning">

- Tax planning has always been a data analytics task
  - Increasingly more complex
- “What-if” scenario modeling
  - Estimating the simultaneous cash-flow and tax effects of different business decisions to jointly optimize
  - Estimating the effects of tax law changes for planning
- Setting transfer prices
  - Data acquisition, benchmarking, documentation

</Slide>

<Slide title="Caveats and Concerns">

- Data quality (up to 80% of time cleaning data)
- Tax is a complex, regulatory based, changing system
  - Models need to be customized, non-generalizable, or complex to account
  - Subjective estimations are difficult to model
- Trust in automated analytical outcomes
  - 66% tax practitioners comfortable with AI providing advice to clients (Thomson Reuters, 2025)

</Slide>
