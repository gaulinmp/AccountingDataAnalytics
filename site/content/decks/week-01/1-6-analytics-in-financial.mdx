<Slide title="Financial Accounting Analytics">

- Preparation of financials
  - Data combination, cleaning, aggregation, error detection and correction
- Financial Statement Analysis (FSA)
- Market returns and volume analyses
- Investor communication analysis

</Slide>

<Slide title="Financial Accounting Data">

- Internal structured data (general ledger, etc.)
- Firm disclosures (conference call, 10-K, Proxy, etc.)
- External accounting data (e.g., Compustat)
- Economic indicators

</Slide>

<Slide title="Financial Statement Preparation">

- Automation of financial close
- Journal entry analysis (100% coverage)
- Accrual estimation and analysis
- Monitoring effectiveness of internal controls
- Automate SOX compliance testing

</Slide>

<Slide title="Financial Statement Analysis">

- Traditionally often limited to simple models (ratios)
- More data and advanced techniques have improved methodologies and increased informativeness
  - Large scale, near real-time data on all firms in the market allow for better benchmarking and insight
  - Machine learning techniques like hyperparameter tuning allow for learning more complex relationships and more accurate predictions

</Slide>

<Slide title="Stakeholder analytics">

- Market research to understand disclosure impacts
  - Example: conference call opening statements are often pre-recorded due to research on voice stress analysis
- Analysis of investor information demand
- Disclosure legal risk from shareholder class-actions

</Slide>

- Analytics adoption at 53% in cost/profit models (Deloitte, 2025)
- Data quality and accessibility

  - Legacy system integration
