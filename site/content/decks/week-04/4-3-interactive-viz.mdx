<Slide title="Interactive Visualization">

- Software to interact with data in predetermined ways
- Set of parameters to adjust, visualization updates accordingly
- Often web-based

<Slide title="Why interactive?">

- Simplify complex analyses by allowing audience agency (and time with the data)
- Allow audience to answer their own questions
  - Facilitates answering of a wider range of questions
  - Allows flexibility when exact objective of audience is unknown
- Facilitate data driven decisions by allowing testing of alternatives, different conditions, “what if” analyses

</Slide>

<Slide title="Common interactions">

- Filtering: select a subset of the data based on characteristics (categories, dates, values, etc.)
- Zooming/scaling: change magnification to see more or less data/visual, and more/less aggregation
- Drill down: navigate hierarchically through data, e.g., total revenue, segment revenue, individual product, etc.
- Parameter adjustment: change inputs into model or visualization, e.g., mean to median, alter growth rate assumptions, etc.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Implementation">

- Business Intelligence platform (Tableau/Power BI): standardized, industry accepted, can handle large datasets/data lakes, costly, less flexible
- DIY (JS/Python): flexible, cheaper, often requires full-stack programming, data size more of an issue (without investment in programming solutions)

</Slide>

<Slide title="IDV In Accounting">

- Auditing: view trends and identify anomalies and outliers for risk assessment
- Forensic: find unusual relationships between entities and observe flow of funds
- Managerial: visualize variance analysis, track costs and bottlenecks, evaluate performance
- Financial: summarize and analyze journal entries, track KPIs, and drill down into summary figures

</Slide>
