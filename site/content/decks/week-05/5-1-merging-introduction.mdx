<Slide title="Why combine data?">

- Myriad data sources
  - External data (marketing data, economic data, etc.)
  - Various internal systems (invoices, production, etc.)
- Aggregation
  - Aggregation can require combining data with itself
- Analysis, cleaning, and verification
  - Comparing observations, interpolating, etc.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Considerations – data sources">

- External data out of your control has unknown quality, can change over time
- Internal data in your control has design decisions that make tradeoffs
- Storage format
- Update frequency
- Access (permissions)

</Slide>

<Slide title="Considerations – Combining data">

- Combining “clean” data on fixed identifiers (unique key) is simple but requires maintaining keys
  - Not always possible with external data
- Many datasets do not have exact matches
  - Vendor names may have abbr., etc.
  - Non-exact matching trades off ability to match with false matches

</Slide>

<Slide title="Other Considerations">

- Repeatability
  - Is the process “one off” or can it be repeated?
- Reliability
  - How robust is the process?
- Automation
  - Can the process be reliably repeated automatically?

</Slide>
