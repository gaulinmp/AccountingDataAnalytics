<Slide title="Financial Accounting">

- General Ledger contains all financial transactions across assets, liabilities, equity, revenues, and expenses
- Territory and calendar tables provide regional and temporal information for reporting
- Merge transactions with geographic location for segment reporting
- Aggregate GL data by account for IS/BS calculations

</Slide>

<Slide title="Tax Accounting">

- Client provided data includes W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, etc.
- Receipts and documentation
- Investment statements contain capital gains, dividends, etc.
- Aggregate investment statements to calculate short- and long-term capital gains/losses
- Merge income data for multi-entity returns

</Slide>

<Slide title="Cost Accounting">

- Each product has a bill of material (BOM)
- Parts are purchased, tracking transactions and invoices
- Manufacturing is tracked with inventory and T-accounts
- Merge products from BOM with purchases with inventory system

</Slide>

<Slide title="Forensic ACcounting">

- General ledger, trial balances, journal entries
- AP/AR records and invoices
- Bank records
- Merge bank records and journal entries to look for mismatches / omitted entries
- Randomly select invoices to verify against GL and bank or cash disbursement records

</Slide>
