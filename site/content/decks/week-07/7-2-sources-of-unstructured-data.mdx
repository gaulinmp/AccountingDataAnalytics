<Slide title="Types of Unstructured data">

- Text
- Audio
- Image

</Slide>

<Slide title="Sources of Unstructured Data">

- Created for primary purpose and used for accounting or analytics purposes
  - Comes with errors or decisions due to primary intent
  - Cost effective
- Created for accounting or analytic purposes
  - Customized, but incurs novel costs to create or collect

</Slide>

<Slide title="Internal Text">

- Communications
  - Emails, texts, memos, social media, presentations
- Data acquisition
  - Reports, logs, surveys
- Filings
  - Annual report (MD&A, Risk Factors, etc.)
  - Earnings announcements, proxies, etc.

</Slide>

<Slide title="External Text">

- Invoices, receipts, legal contracts, regulations
- Regulations, regulatory investigations
- Competitor filings
  - MD&A, product descriptions, risk factors, etc.
- Audit clients
  - Working papers, internal controls evidence
- Tax clients
  - W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, receipts, income statements

</Slide>

<Slide title="Image / Video Data">

- Documents scanned/photographed (pre-OCR)
- Production / inventory evidence
  - Manufacturing QC image analysis
  - Drone inventory tracking
- Meeting or presentation recordings

</Slide>

<Slide title="Semi-structured data">

- Data with some format
  - XML, JSON, etc.
- Often requires feature extraction
- Often requires less transforming from source format into usable structured format

</Slide>
