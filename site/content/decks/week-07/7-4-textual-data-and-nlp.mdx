<Slide title="Text and Computers">

- Text is stored as numbers, usually by letter
  - In ASCII, “A” is 65, “B” is 66, “a” is 97, and so on.
- To perform analysis on text, we need some better representation of text
- “Preprocessing” is the task of converting text into something usable for analytical purposes

</Slide>

<Slide title="Natural Language Processing">

- NLP addresses understanding language to process and analyze texts
  - E.g. text classification, information retrieval, speech recognition
- Old approach: developed rules (grammars) for parsing text
- New approach: lots of math (machine learning)

</Slide>

<Slide title="Preprocessing">

- Tokenization: breaking text into tokens (letters, words, sentences, or whatever is needed)
  - “University of Utah”  [“University”, “of”, “Utah]
- Cleaning: (optional) lowercase, remove punctuation, add placeholders (e.g. numbers, dates)
  - “FYEnd, 12/31/2025”  [“fyend”, “DATE”]
- Stemming: replacing words with their “root” by removing conjugations and plurality
  - “She abhors assumptions”  [“she”, “abhor”, “assum”]

</Slide>

<Slide title="Preprocessing Caveats">

- Preprocessing removes information
  - Uppercase letters can be informative (May vs may)
  - Punctuation is often important
  - Conjugation is almost always important
- When using for analytics, preprocessing is critical for the signal-to-noise tradeoff
  - Removing noise if case, punctuation, conjugation etc. aren’t important
  - Removing information if they are

</Slide>

<Slide title="Vectorization">

- Turning text into numbers
  - “See spot Run. Run Spot run”
  - [see, 2 * spot, 3 * run]
  - &lt;1, 2, 3, 0, …, 0>
- What if we want to keep word the sense of words being together (i.e. word order)
  - [“see spot”, “spot run”, “run spot”, “spot run”] (“run run”?)
  - &lt;1, 2, 1, 0, …, 0>

</Slide>

<Slide title="N-Grams">

- Vector of words that are N-tokens long
- Many more bi-grams than unigrams
  - From my paper on Executive Compensation:
    - # of 1-grams: 238,001
    - # of 2-grams: 2,230,332 (9.4x)
    - # of 3-grams: 3,459,221 (1.55x)
    - # of 4-grams: 5,386,503 (1.56x)

</Slide>

<Slide title="Vector Weighting">

- Could count words
  - “See spot Run. Run Spot run”  &lt;1, 2, 3>
- Could weigh words by importance
  -  &lt;1 * 0.1, 2 * 0.5, 3 * 0.33>  &lt;0.1, 1, 1>
- Term Frequency – Inverse Document Frequency
  - = # times word occurs / # times word occurs in all docs
  - Common word (the) in 1000 docs  # / 1000
  - Rare word (synecdoche) in 1 doc  # / 1

</Slide>

<Slide title="NLP in Accounting">

- Information retrieval: extracting specific information or facts from documents
- Entity linking: identifying people/places/things and linking them to known entities
- Relationship extraction: extracting relationships between things or ideas
  - Ex: company  product, event  date
- Topic analysis & sentiment analysis

</Slide>
