<Slide title="“Spaces”">

- Word-embedding embeds words into a space
- The magic is that the space seems to encode meaning, relations between words
- This extends to sentence embeddings, thought embeddings, etc.
- NLP techniques now transform word space into idea space
  - This is what LLMs do and it’s magical

</Slide>

<Slide title="Large Language Models">

- LLMs operate by repeatedly predicting the next ____
- First step is to embed the input
- They then run a sequence of neural network layers
  - Attention: share information across words and determine what is important
  - Feedforward: store information (we think?!) for understanding
- Lastly, predict a new word that “should” come next
- Repeat with the new predicted word as the last word

</Slide>

<Slide title="LLM Caveats">

- Very new
- LLMs seem to be good at pattern recognition
  - This can look like thinking, but it is not (yet)
- Captures the average based on its training data

</Slide>

<Slide title="NLP in the LLM Era">

- Very new
- It all comes down to the prompt (for now)
- Very good at:
  - Named entity recognition, relation extraction, sentiment
- Middling good at:
  - Entity linking, fact retrieval
- Unknown good at:
  - Topic analysis, drawing conclusions

</Slide>

<Slide title="NLP in the LLM Era">

- LLMs may be overkill
- LLMs are far more black-box
  - Dependent on training data, fine-tuning data, but to an unknown extent
- Still room for older approaches
  - When it does what it does well
  - When a (more) deterministic outcome is necessary

</Slide>
