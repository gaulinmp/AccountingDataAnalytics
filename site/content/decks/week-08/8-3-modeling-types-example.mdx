<Slide title="Model Intent Timeline">

- Identify the situation and what occurred (descriptive)
- Determine why it occurred the way it did (diagnostic)
- Leverage that knowledge to predict outcomes outside the specific situation (predictive)
- Consider different outcomes and choose one for some (good) reason (prescriptive)

</Slide>

<Slide title="Example – Accounts Receivable">

- GAAP treatment for uncollectable AR (bad debt)
  - Historical %: % bad debt / % credit sales
  - AR Aging: % bad debt varies by AR age
  - Risk classification: assess risk based on customer characteristics

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 1: Descriptive Analysis">

- Collect historical data on sales, credit sales, and uncollected AR, and account characteristics
- Calculate number and portion of AR that were uncollected for percent of sales method
- Calculate age of AR for aging AR method
- Merge account characteristics and calculate their summary statistics by AR type / age

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 2: Diagnostic Analysis">

- Which approach to use?
- Study AR collectability variance (e.g. by AR age)
- Investigate AR collection probability
  - Look for time-series trends (more default during holidays?)
  - Look for product or vendor correlations
  - Correlate account characteristics with collected status

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 3: Predictive Analysis">

- Which accounts will default?
- Refine AR collection probability model
  - Compare various methodological approaches
  - Select features (use your accounting expertise!)
  - Test model on new data (called the test set)
  - Study assumption, inputs, and characteristics of model

</Slide>

<Slide title="Step 4: Prescriptive analysis">

- What should we do about bad debt?
  - GAAP treatment isn’t the business goal
- Consider what options are available
  - Often limited to what was modeled
- Consider costs and benefits of any changes
  - E.g. changing AR terms for some customers may result in loss of business, balance that against cost of default
- Consider externalities and un-modeled impacts

</Slide>
