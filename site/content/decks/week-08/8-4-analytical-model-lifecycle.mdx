<Slide title="Modeling Lifecycle">

- Iterative process
  - Continually consider changing or refining previous stages
  - Once deployed, define new features or improvements

</Slide>

<Slide title="Define the Goal">

- What question or decision do we have?
  - What AR are uncollectable?
  - Whether to impair goodwill or not
- Define the scope of the question
  - What decisions will the modeling inform?
  - How will we measure success?
  - What is the timeline?

</Slide>

<Slide title="Define the Goal">

- Define the constraints
  - What can/cannot be concluded from the results?
  - What are the limitations of the model?
- Define the concerns and risks
  - What’s the cost of being “wrong”? (error cost)
  - How “wrong” is acceptable? (error rates)
  - What are potential biases in the model or data?

</Slide>

<Slide title="Collect the Data">

- Identify necessary data to fit model
- Consider limitations of data
  - Do they affect what the model can address?
- Automate collection where possible
- Document data sources, access methods, dates
- Go back and update the goal definition step depending on data availability, quality, etc.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Develop and fit the Model">

- Choose model type to fit goal
  - Descriptive, diagnostic, predictive or prescriptive?
  - Simple KPI dashboard, historical average, neural net?
- Consider “cost” of model
  - False positives/negatives: cost of being wrong?
  - Prediction error: how wrong could we be?
- Tune the model to fit the data
  - Feature engineering, K-fold validation, train/test sets

</Slide>

<Slide title="Evaluate Performance">

- Determine how well the model performs
  - Back-testing: applying model to historical data
  - Sensitivity analysis: how much does outcome change if inputs are changed (e.g. interest rates, costs, etc.)?
  - Accuracy: RMSE, AUC, confusion matrix, etc.
- Assess economic impact
  - Were predictions of risks or costs/benefits accurate?
- Update previous steps given assessed performance

</Slide>

<Slide title="Deploy the model">

- Put the model into use
  - Automated if possible, e.g. dashboards
  - Requiring minimal interactions if not, e.g. pull new data manually, then run the rest automatically
- Continual evaluation
  - Economic effects likely not realized for some time
- Re-evaluate from step 1 with regularity

</Slide>
