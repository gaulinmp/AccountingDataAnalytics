<Slide title="Example: Calculating Cost Functions">

- Our task is to calculate more accurate product costs
- Differentiating between product and period costs is difficult
- Outline: define, collect, model, evaluate, deploy

</Slide>

<Slide title="Define the goal">

- Estimate product costs across firm’s offerings
  - ETL combining purchase records, inventory, overheads
  - Develop cost prediction model  this video’s focus
  - Create reports and dashboards to communicate historical costs and forecast future costs for planning
- Outcome is calculations of average costs, in the realm of diagnostic modeling
  - Potential future expansion for predictive costing

</Slide>

<Slide title="Data: Collection">

- Data from internal systems
  - Purchase records for materials
  - Payroll records for labour
  - Expense records for overheads
  - Inventory system for accrual tracking

</Slide>

<Slide title="Model: Choosing a model">

- Production costs likely stepwise linear
  - Potential concavity (economies of scale)
  - Potential convexity (increasing costs)
- Try simple linear model to start (OLS)
  - Interpretable coefficients lead to differentiating between variable and fixed costs
  - Allows cost prediction as production volume changes

</Slide>

<Slide title="Modeling: Fitting the model">

- Start with most simple approach: TC = FC + VC * X
  - We know TC and X (volume), predicting fixed and variable costs

<ImageCaption src="/decks/week-09/9-2-regression-example/img1.png" alt="Modeling: Fitting the model" />

</Slide>

<Slide title="Evaluation">

- Investigate how well first pass at model did
  - R2: how much of our total cost did we explain?
  - RMSE: what was our average error in dollars?
- First model was an average cost based on volume
- Do we want more detail?
  - Break out product inputs?
  - Break out each product?

</Slide>

<Slide title="Model 2.0: Break out inputs">

- Instead of volume, let’s use inputs (broad categories)
- TC = FC + VCL*DL + VCM*DM + VCV*VOH
- DL: amount of labor, in hours
- DM: amount of material, e.g. by weight
- VOH: amount of variable overhead, by cost driver

</Slide>

<Slide title="Model 2.0: Interpretation">

- FC estimate tells us our average fixed cost in dollars
- VC estimates tell us our $ of cost per driver
  - E.g. average $ / labour hour
- These estimates are averages, but we could add more terms to model to do less aggregating
  - E.g. multiple DMs, e.g. metal and plastic separately

</Slide>

<Slide title="Deploy">

- Apply parameter estimates to the amount of input each product requires
  - E.g. VCM * material per widget
- These estimates could be used to cost out future products and perform NPV analyses

</Slide>

<Slide title="Iterate">

- Consider further developing model to more closely mirror the true economic production function
  - E.g. explicitly model semi-fixed costs like PP&E
- Test predictions against cost-saving measures to estimate out of sample / predictive accuracy
  - Fix issues where you identify them
- Further improve model as more data comes in

</Slide>
