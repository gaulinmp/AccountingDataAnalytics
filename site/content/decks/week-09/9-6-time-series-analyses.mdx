<Slide title="What is Time-series data">

- Order matters

- Analyzing transaction trends over time
- Modeling crash risk, survival analysis
- Predicting demand for inventory management

<Slide title="Stationarity – So what?">

- Analytics on a non-stationary will often be wrong
  - A non-stationary process needs different math
- A non-stationary process could be “made” stationary
  - This is why we often study returns, not market cap

<Slide title="Time-Series as supervised learning">

- Predict Sales at time t (Salest) with Salest-1, Salest-2
- ARIMA model
  - AR=autoregression: predicting Sales with past Sales
  - I=integrated: solve the pesky non-stationary problem
  - MA=moving average: surprises persist over time
- Could add other features (Interestt-1, GDPt-1, etc.)
  - E.g., SARIMAX adds seasonality and other features

</Slide>

<Slide title="Time-series with neural networks">

- Like traditional methods, but fancier
- Pre-trained neural networks on time-series data
  - E.g., TimesFM from Google, trained on 100 billion observations of real-world time-series data
  - Unclear if or when these approaches are better
- Training a complex model requires huge datasets
  - With less data, parametric models generally dominate

</Slide>

<Slide title="Cautions">

- Training data could economic events that won’t occur in the future (overfitting)
- New economic events that aren’t modeled can occur (underfitting)
- Uncertainty increases when forecasting further out
