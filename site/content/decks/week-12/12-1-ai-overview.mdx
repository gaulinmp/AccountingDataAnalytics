<Slide title="What is AI">

- Technical definition: something that performs tasks normally done by a human
  - E.g., reasoning, decision making, creating, etc.
- Colloquial definition: a computer doing something neat or difficult

<ImageCaption src="/decks/week-12/12-1-ai-overview/img1.png" alt="What is AI" />

- Credit: nasa.gov/what-is-artificial-intelligence

</Slide>

<Slide title="AI in 2025">

- Highly capable rule or statistical based systems
  - E.g., deep neural networks like AlphaGo
- Foundation models: generalized models trained on vast data that can be fine-tuned for various tasks
  - Chat interface: GPT-3
  - Multimodal chat interface: GPT-4+, Gemini, Claude
  - Image/video generation: Dall-E, Sora, Veo

</Slide>

<Slide title="Is AI intelligent?">

- Mostly an academic question
- More importantly, what does AI do well, and what does it struggle at?
  - Good: giving you what you ask for (often only that)
  - Bad: originality, novelty, surprise
- LLMs are trained on the gamut of recorded humanity
  - “History doesn't repeat itself, but it often rhymes.”
  - ~ probably not Mark Twain

</Slide>
