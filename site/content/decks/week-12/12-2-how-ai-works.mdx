<Slide title="How neural networks learn">

- Calculate an output Y’ from inputs X
- Calculate error (e.g. Y – Y’)
- Backpropegate this error from the last layer through the first layer, updating the weights and biases based on that neuron’s error
- Repeat millions of times

</Slide>

<Slide title="Types of Neural Networks">

- Feed-forward NN: what we’ve seen so far
- Convolutional NN: good for images & videos
- Recurrent NN: add “memory” to neuron’s inputs
  - Long Short-Term Memory, Gated Recurrent Unit
- Auto-encoder: encode and decode information for compression / dimensionality reduction
- Transformer: attention made all the difference

</Slide>

<Slide title="Transformers: Chat GPT">

- Text is broken into “tokens”
  - E.g., shareholder equity = assets - liabilities
- Tokens are embedded
- Vectors are passed through series of attention heads
  - Attention: previous words “modify” the current one
  - Multilayer Perceptron: adds learned information (facts)
  - Each attention layer is many attention heads in parallel
- Final network is many attention layers in a row

</Slide>

<Slide title="Large Language/Multimodal Models">

- The next token is predicted based on only the final embedding in the network
  - E.g. GPT-3 predicts the next token with only 12,287 of the more than 175 billion parameters in the model
- Trained on the internet of data
- Output is every token’s probability of being next
  - Next token is randomly chosen, based on its probability
  - Temperature: less likely tokens more often selected
  - Top P: how many tokens are considered*

- * Mathematically this isn’t true, but effectively (and non-linearly) it can be thought of this way.

</Slide>

<Slide title="Wrong or Hallucinating?">

- No sense of “fact” or “truth”, just learned patterns
- Generates the next token given the entirety of what was learned during training and the rest of your prompt (including chat history)
- Tokens are predicted one at a time
  - Randomly choosing a low probability word (high temp) can set the generation off on a very different path
  - A drunkard’s walk rather than moving towards a target

</Slide>
