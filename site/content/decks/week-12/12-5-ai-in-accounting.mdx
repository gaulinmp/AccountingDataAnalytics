<Slide title="AI in Accounting – Recap">

- Automation, Robotic Process Automation (RPA)
  - Data ingest, cleaning, merging, quality control
  - Continual process monitoring (i.e., internal controls)
- Comprehensive analysis of all transactions
- Detecting anomalies, misstatements, fraud, etc.
- Audit trail semantic search
- Forecasting and predictive modeling

</Slide>

<Slide title="AI Benefits">

- Error reduction and accuracy
  - Automating mundane tasks with reliable software
- Work efficiency and productivity1
  - –1 week month-end close time
  - +21% billable hours, +55% clients supported
- Augmenting human judgement
  - Providing more easily consumable data for decisions

- 1 https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5240924

</Slide>

<Slide title="Barriers to Adoption">

- Data security & privacy
  - 36% employees admit to using non-approved LLM
- Accuracy & data
  - Mitigating hallucinations
  - Acquiring useful, clean, and reliable data

<Slide title="AI Adoption is Accelerating">

- More so among larger businesses than SMEs
- In 2024, 78% of organizations have implemented AI in some business function, up from 55% (McKinsey, 2024)
  - 70% use AI content without review
- 50% of tax & accounting professionals currently use AI or plan to within the year (Wolters Kluwer, 2025)
- 70% of financial reporting functions using AI (KPMG, 2024)

</Slide>

<Slide title="Planned AI Applications">

- Assisting with summarizing accounting policy and legal documents
- Evaluating the completeness of audit documentation against relevant documentation requirements
- Performing certain risk assessment procedures
- Scoping the audit
- Evaluating the completeness of financial statement disclosures
- Comparing amounts in the financial statements or notes with audited amounts

</Slide>
